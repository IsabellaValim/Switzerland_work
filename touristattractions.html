Tourist attractions

Matterhorn (Zermatt)

One of the most iconic peaks in the Alps, with a pyramid-shaped summit. Very popular among skiers, climbers, and photographers.

Jungfraujoch – Top of Europe

The highest railway station in Europe. Spectacular views of the Alps, glaciers, and snow all year round.

Lake Geneva

One of the largest lakes in Western Europe. Cities like Geneva, Montreux, and Lausanne are located along its shores.

Lucerne and the Chapel Bridge (Kapellbrücke)

A medieval city with one of the oldest covered wooden bridges in Europe. Stunning views of the Alps in the background.

Interlaken

Nestled between Lake Thun and Lake Brienz, it’s a paradise for adventure sports and Alpine excursions.

Chillon Castle (Montreux)

A medieval castle on the shores of Lake Geneva. One of the most visited historical monuments in Switzerland.

Bern – Old Town

Switzerland’s capital with a well-preserved medieval old town. A UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Zurich

The largest city in Switzerland. Blends modern urban life with historic buildings, museums, and the beautiful Lake Zurich.

Lauterbrunnen Region

A valley with 72 waterfalls, charming villages, and amazing hiking trails. Close to villages like Wengen and Mürren.

St. Moritz Mountains

A world-famous luxury ski resort. Host of two Winter Olympic Games.
